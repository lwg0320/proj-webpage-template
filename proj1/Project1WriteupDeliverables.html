Task 5 (15 pts)

  • Explain pixel sampling in your own words and describe how you implemented it to perform texture mapping. Briefly discuss the two different pixel sampling methods, nearest and bilinear.
  • Nearest sampling is when we sample based on the closest pixel in the texture based on what the mapped uv coordinates are.
  • Bilinear sampling is a more sophisticated nearest sampling method where we take 4 nearest points and perform two rounds of linear interpolation to determine the pixel value.

Task 6 (25 pts)

  • Explain level sampling in your own words and describe how you implemented it for texture mapping.
  • Level sampling is when we create differing sizes of maps of images with smaller and smaller maps, representing a lower resolution of the original image (mipmaps). By using these maps, we can have 3 ways to sample – L_ZERO, L_NEAREST, L_LINEAR.
  • You can now adjust your sampling technique by selecting pixel sampling, level sampling, or the number of samples per pixel. Describe the tradeoffs between speed, memory usage, and antialiasing power between the three various techniques.
  • Speed: Linear takes the longest, then nearest, then lastly zero.
  • Memory: Linear should take the most memory because it samples from more than one mipmap level whereas zero and nearest only sample from one.
  • Antialiasing power: We want to use supersampling/trilinear sampling for the most antialiasing power. Whereas nearest doesn't really help with antialiasing power.
